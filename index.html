Product Sense

How I think about products

A live product teardown of two UAE-relevant products — the kind of analysis I'd bring on day one.

🛒
Noon.com — Search & Discovery Teardown E-commerce · UAE market · How I'd approach improving it
Problem observed

Search results on Noon lack personalisation signals — two users searching "running shoes" get identical ranked results regardless of past behaviour, price sensitivity, or brand affinity. Zero-result rates on Arabic queries appear high. Recommendations on PDP feel generic.

Hypotheses to validate
  • Arabic query understanding is weaker than English — causing higher abandonment for MENA-native users
  • No implicit personalisation signals used in ranking — browse, cart, wishlist history ignored
  • Cross-sell on PDP is category-level, not user-intent-level
  • Zero-result query rate likely above 15% for colloquial Arabic terms
Recommendations
  • Implement bilingual NLP query understanding with Arabic entity normalisation
  • Layer implicit personalisation on search ranking (view history, price range affinity)
  • Build zero-result fallback with semantic "did you mean" suggestions
  • A/B test personalised vs. popularity-ranked results — measure CTR + conversion delta
  • Success metric: zero-result rate, CTR on top-3, revenue per search session
📺
Shahid (MBC Group) — Content Discovery Teardown OTT Streaming · MENA market · How I'd improve retention through discovery
Problem observed

Shahid's home screen shows heavy editorial curation but limited algorithmic personalisation. Users returning after 7+ days see near-identical rows. Search is basic keyword matching — no semantic understanding. High Ramadan traffic spikes likely mask retention drop-off post-season.

Hypotheses to validate
  • Post-Ramadan retention falls sharply — no content discovery mechanism to hook users off-season
  • Users can't articulate what they want in search — they explore, not search. Home UX doesn't serve explorers
  • Content graph (cast, genre, theme, mood) is likely underutilised in recommendations
  • 7-day and 30-day retention cohorts are likely weak outside top-10 content
Recommendations
  • Build "Because you watched X" row using content graph similarity + user vectors
  • Introduce mood/occasion-based discovery (Ramadan content → family drama → binge-worthy Arabic series)
  • Add semantic search with Arabic NLP — entity extraction for actors, directors, themes
  • Instrument 7-day retention by entry point — search vs. recommendations vs. editorial — to find highest-retention discovery path
  • Success metric: 7-day and 30-day retention, session frequency, search-to-play rate

AI is a capability,
not a feature.

The best AI products I've built are invisible to users. Nobody thinks "this is an ML model" — they think "this app just gets me." That's the bar I hold myself to: measurable user outcomes, not model sophistication. Every AI investment I've championed had a clear hypothesis, a defined success metric, and an off-ramp if it didn't work.

01
Start with user intent, not model capability. What does the user actually want to accomplish?
02
Define success metrics before building. Engagement lift, CTR, or retention — pick the one that matters most.
03
Build in feedback loops. AI products degrade without data pipelines that keep models fresh and accurate.
04
Ship fast, learn faster. A/B tests over long development cycles — always, without exception.

Product Thinking

How I approach problems

Frameworks I've built from real work — not textbook theory.

Search quality diagnostic framework

My structured approach to diagnosing any search or discovery surface — applicable to e-commerce, streaming, or marketplace products. I've used this at all three companies.

1
Diagnose with four signalsZero-result rate, CTR on top results, query reformulation rate, and abandon rate. These tell me exactly where the search experience is breaking.
2
Classify user intentNavigational (find X), informational (show me Y genre), transactional (buy/subscribe). Each requires a fundamentally different ranking strategy.
3
Layer personalisation carefullyImplicit signals (history, behaviour) trump explicit (ratings, preferences). Personalise ranking, not just recommendation rows.
4
Experiment, never assumeHypothesis → A/B test → statistical significance → ship or kill. Never gut-feel a ranking change at scale.
5
Monitor for model decayCatalogue changes, seasonality, and data drift degrade quality. Set automated KPI alerts — not quarterly reviews.

My PRD structure — 5 questions

Every PRD I write answers these five questions before a single feature is specified. Engineering never starts without all five answered.

1
What problem are we solving?User evidence: quotes, data, support tickets. No assumptions accepted.
2
Who is the primary user?One persona, not six. If it's for everyone, it's for no one.
3
What does success look like?One primary metric, two secondary. Defined before engineering starts.
4
What are we NOT doing?Explicit exclusions prevent scope creep better than any process.
5
How will we learn fast?Phased rollout plan, kill-switch criteria, feedback loop — upfront.
